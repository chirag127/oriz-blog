# Part 7: Weather Readiness and Combating the May Heat

Traveling to Puri in May is a physical endurance test. The coastal climate means you are dealing not just with high temperatures, but with oppressive, energy-draining humidity. You asked specifically about the weather for tomorrow, May 9, 2026, and what gear you should carry. 

Here is your survival guide to the Puri weather.

## The May 9, 2026 Forecast

*   **Temperature:** Expect a high of **33°C to 35°C**, but the "Feels Like" temperature (due to high humidity) will easily touch **39°C to 41°C**.
*   **Low:** Around 27°C in the early morning.
*   **Precipitation:** 0% chance of rain during the day. It will be bright, sunny, and intense.
*   **UV Index:** Extreme (11+). The sun will be punishing between 10:00 AM and 3:00 PM.

## The Essential Packing List

Because you cannot carry a bag into the temple, what you take from Bhubaneswar must be strategic. You need to protect your mother from heatstroke while carrying minimal baggage.

### 1. The Umbrella (Crucial)
You must carry a sturdy umbrella. While you cannot take it *inside* the temple sanctum, you will need it while walking the Bada Danda from your auto drop-off point to the gates, and later when visiting the beach or Ananda Bazar. Share a large, dark-colored umbrella that provides solid UV protection.

### 2. Caps and Hats
A wide-brimmed hat or a simple cotton cap is highly recommended for the walk. Again, you will likely have to remove this (or hold it in your hand) when entering the actual temple premises out of respect, but it will save you on the streets.

### 3. Sunscreen
Apply a high-SPF (50+) sunscreen before you leave your home in Niladri Vihar. Reapply it if you carry a small tube in your pocket. The coastal sun burns skin rapidly.

### 4. Water Bottles
*   **The Rule:** You **cannot** carry your own water bottle into the main temple complex.
*   **The Strategy:** Hydrate heavily on the train. Buy a cold bottle of water right after getting off the auto near the temple and drink it before joining the queue. Once inside, the temple provides earthen pots filled with drinking water, and there are RO water points. 

### 5. The "Sweat Towel"
This is the most underrated item for a May trip to Puri. Carry a small, thin, cotton hand towel (locally called a *Gamcha* fabric towel). The humidity inside the crowded temple halls is intense, and having a towel to wipe sweat will keep you and your mother much more comfortable.

### 6. ORS / Glucon-D Packets
Carry two small packets of ORS powder in your pocket. If your mother feels dizzy or dehydrated after the darshan, you can immediately buy a bottle of water outside, mix the powder, and restore her electrolytes. 

## What to Wear: The Fabric Factor

We covered the modesty rules in Part 4 (Dhoti/Kurta for you, Saree/Salwar for your mother), but the *material* is just as important as the style.

*   **100% Cotton Only:** Do not wear synthetic blends, nylon, or heavy silk. They will trap heat and make you miserable. Wear light, breathable, pure cotton clothes.
*   **Light Colors:** White, light blue, and pastel shades reflect the sun.
*   **Loose Fitting:** Ensure the clothing is loose to allow air circulation.

## Managing the Hot Temple Floors

By 11:00 AM, the stone courtyards of the temple complex bake in the sun. Walking barefoot on these stones can cause blisters.
*   **Follow the Carpets:** The temple administration lays down coir mats and sprays water on designated walking paths. **Strictly stick to these wet mats.** Do not try to take a shortcut across the open, sun-baked stone. 

Surviving the heat makes the reward even sweeter. After the intense darshan and the hot walk, there is nothing more satisfying than sitting down for a holy meal. In Part 8, we explore the legendary Ananda Bazar and the Mahaprasad experience.
