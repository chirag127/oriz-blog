# Part 5: Security, Lockers, and The Digital Detox

The Shree Jagannath Temple is not just a spiritual center; it is a high-security zone. Following historical threats and a strict adherence to preserving the sanctity of the deities, the temple administration enforces a complete ban on modern gadgets and specific materials. 

This means your visit to the temple will be a forced, yet beautiful, "Digital Detox."

## The "Absolutely Not" List (Prohibited Items)

You asked specific questions about what to carry and what to avoid. Here is the definitive guide to what will get you stopped at the metal detectors:

1.  **Mobile Phones:** Strictly banned. Do not even try to sneak a small phone in; the frisking is thorough.
2.  **Cameras:** All forms of cameras (DSLRs, point-and-shoots, GoPros) are banned. The inner sanctum has never been photographed, and the temple intends to keep it that way.
3.  **Smartwatches:** This is a recent addition. Any watch that has a screen, Bluetooth, or camera capabilities (Apple Watches, Galaxy Watches, fitness trackers) is prohibited. Wear a standard, analog watch or no watch at all.
4.  **Earbuds/Headphones:** Banned.
5.  **Laptops and Tablets:** Absolutely banned. **Do not carry a laptop to Puri.** If you are doing a day trip, leave your laptop at home in Niladri Vihar. 
6.  **Power Banks:** Banned. 
7.  **Leather Items:** Belts, wallets, and bags made of genuine leather are considered impure and are strictly prohibited. Use a cloth wallet or a nylon/canvas bag. If you wear a leather belt, you will have to remove it at the gate.
8.  **Water Bottles:** You cannot carry your own plastic or metal water bottle inside. Drinking water facilities (earthen pots and RO systems) are available inside the temple complex.
9.  **Weapons/Sharp Objects:** Naturally, knives, scissors, and matchboxes are banned.

## Where Are the Lockers?

Since you must deposit your phone and prohibited items, knowing where to do so safely is critical.

*   **Official Cloakrooms:** The temple administration runs official mobile and baggage deposit counters immediately outside the main gates, notably near the *Singha Dwar* (Lion's Gate) and the *Ashwa Dwar* (Horse Gate). 
*   **The Cost:** These official lockers are highly subsidized. You will pay a nominal fee ranging from **₹5 to ₹20** depending on the items deposited.
*   **The Process:** You hand over your items, pay the fee, and receive a metal or plastic token. **Guard this token with your life.** Without it, retrieving your phone becomes a bureaucratic nightmare.
*   **Private Lockers:** Surrounding the temple, dozens of private shops sell flowers and prasad. Many offer "free" mobile lockers if you buy a large puja thali from them. While convenient, it is always safer to use the **official, government-run counters** for expensive items like phones.

## What Should You Actually Carry?

When stepping into the temple queue, your pockets should be incredibly light. Here is all you need:

1.  **Small Cash:** Carry notes of ₹10, ₹20, ₹50, and ₹100. You will need cash for the shoe stand, mobile locker, buying *diyas* (lamps), making small offerings at the numerous smaller shrines, and purchasing Mahaprasad at Ananda Bazar. **Digital payments (UPI/Cards) are useless inside the temple.**
2.  **Aadhaar Card:** As discussed in Part 4, keep it safely in your pocket.
3.  **A Handkerchief or Small Towel:** May in Puri is extremely humid. You will sweat profusely. A small cotton towel is a lifesaver.
4.  **A Cloth Pouch:** To hold your cash and ID securely.

By leaving laptops, smartwatches, and leather belts back in Bhubaneswar, you streamline your entry process, saving you and your mother the stress of arguing with security guards or waiting in long cloakroom queues.
