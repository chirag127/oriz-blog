# Part 2: Train Route Blueprint and Saturday Morning Strategy

Following the master plan laid out in Part 1, arriving at the Bhubaneswar Railway Station by 5:30 AM positions you perfectly to catch an early train. The railway journey to Puri is not just a commute; it is a historic passage. The Khurda Road-Puri branch line was opened to traffic in 1897, revolutionizing the pilgrimage to the Jagannath Temple. Before this, devotees would walk for days or weeks, facing immense hardships. Today, the train remains the most democratic, comfortable, and efficient way to reach the holy city.

## Why Saturday is Crucial

You are traveling on Saturday, May 9, 2026. Saturdays in Puri are inherently crowded. It is a weekend, and many locals from Cuttack, Bhubaneswar, and surrounding districts make day trips. By choosing to travel early in the morning, you are executing a brilliant strategy to arrive before the massive influx of tourists hits the *Bada Danda* around 10:00 AM.

### The Recommended Train: Durg Puri Express (18426) or a Local MEMU/Passenger

If you reach Bhubaneswar station by 5:30 AM, you will likely find local Passenger or MEMU trains that are not always listed on long-distance schedules but run frequently in the morning (e.g., the 5:45 AM or 6:15 AM local trains). 

However, if you want a guaranteed, scheduled express train, the **Durg Puri Express at 6:45 AM** is excellent. It allows you a relaxed transit from Niladri Vihar, time to grab a cup of tea at the station, and a comfortable ride, dropping you in Puri by 9:30 AM.

### Should You Go the Night Before?

You asked: *"Is it a better idea to go in the night of the 8th of May... and go into the line of the temple at very morning?"*

Going the night before (May 8th) and staying up all night or booking a hotel just for a few hours of sleep is generally **not recommended** when traveling with your mother. The physical exhaustion of a sleepless night, combined with the extreme heat of May and the long queues of the temple, will drain her energy rapidly. The 6:45 AM train on Saturday strikes the perfect balance—you get a full night's sleep in your own bed and still reach early enough to beat the worst of the midday heat.

## Ticketing and Costs

*   **General Ticket:** Go to the unreserved ticketing counter (UTS) at Bhubaneswar station. A general ticket costs roughly **₹45 to ₹60**. 
*   **The UTS App:** To avoid queues at the station, download the UTS (Unreserved Ticketing System) mobile app by Indian Railways. You can book a paperless ticket on your phone while on your way to the station in the auto. This is a massive time-saver.

## The Journey Experience

The train ride takes you through the lush, green landscapes of coastal Odisha. As you pass through Khurda Road and Delang, the anticipation builds. There is zero risk of motion sickness, making it the perfect start for your mother. Use this time to mentally prepare, organize your small cash, and ensure your Aadhaar cards are accessible.
