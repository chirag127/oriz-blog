# Part 3: Arrival at Puri, Distances, and Rickshaw Navigation

As your train pulls into the Puri Railway Station, you have officially arrived in the abode of Lord Jagannath. The station itself is steeped in history, featuring architecture inspired by the temple. However, the moment you step out of the station, you will be swarmed by auto-rickshaw drivers and touts offering rides and "special darshan" packages. 

This is where your preparation pays off.

## Distances: Knowing Your Geography

Before you negotiate with any driver, you must know the actual distances. This prevents you from being overcharged.

*   **Puri Railway Station to Jagannath Temple (Singha Dwar):** Approximately **2.5 to 3 kilometers**.
*   **Puri Bus Stand to Jagannath Temple:** Approximately **1.5 to 2 kilometers**.
*   **Puri Railway Station to Puri Beach (Swargadwar):** Approximately **2.5 kilometers**.

The temple and the beach are roughly parallel to each other, with the Grand Road (*Bada Danda*) acting as the main spine connecting the temple to the rest of the city.

## Navigating from the Station to the Temple

You have two main options for covering the 3-kilometer stretch to the temple:

### 1. Auto-Rickshaws (The Traditional Route)

Outside the station, traditional auto-rickshaws are abundant. 
*   **The Cost:** For a direct ride to the temple, drivers will initially quote anywhere from ₹150 to ₹250, identifying you as a tourist. 
*   **The Real Fare:** The fair price for a reserved auto from the station to the temple is **₹50 to ₹100** maximum. 
*   **Negotiation Tip:** Politely but firmly state your price. Do not get angry; it is just business. If one driver refuses, walk a few steps towards the main road, and another will agree. Ensure you clarify that the fare is *for the whole auto*, not *per person*.

### 2. E-Rickshaws (Totos) - The Budget Friendly Choice

In recent years, battery-operated E-rickshaws (locally called Totos) have become the lifeline of Puri's local transport.
*   **The Cost:** They operate on a shared basis. The cost is fixed at **₹20 to ₹30 per person**.
*   **The Experience:** They are silent, eco-friendly, and perfect for the narrow, crowded streets of Puri. They are highly recommended for you and your mother. Just hop on one heading towards "Mandir" or "Bada Danda".

## The Final Stretch: The Grand Road Restriction

It is crucial to know that vehicles are **not allowed directly up to the temple gates**. The police barricade the Grand Road (*Bada Danda*) several hundred meters before the temple to manage crowd flow. 

Your auto or E-rickshaw will drop you at a designated point (often near the Town Police Station or Marichikote Chhak). From this drop-off point, you and your mother will have to **walk the last 400 to 500 meters** to the main entrance, the *Singha Dwar* (Lion's Gate). 

### Walking the Bada Danda

This walk is an essential part of the pilgrimage. The wide avenue is flanked by mathas (monasteries), old buildings, and hundreds of shops selling everything from tulsi beads to traditional brassware. 

**Pro-Tip for Your Mother:** If your mother has difficulty walking this final half-kilometer, you can hire a cycle-rickshaw for this specific stretch. Cycle-rickshaws are often allowed slightly further down the road than motorized vehicles.

As you walk, the majestic 214-foot tall *Nila Chakra* (Blue Wheel) atop the temple spire will come into view, dominating the skyline. It is a breathtaking sight that instantly instills a sense of devotion.
