# Part 10: Relaxation, Puri Beach, and the Journey Back Home

After a spiritually fulfilling and physically demanding day navigating the Jagannath Temple, Ananda Bazar, and local museums, the late afternoon calls for relaxation. The Bay of Bengal offers the perfect backdrop to decompress before heading back to your home in Niladri Vihar, Bhubaneswar.

## Should I Visit Puri Beach?

You asked: *"Should I visit Puri Beach or not? What other things to try?"*

**Yes, absolutely.** However, timing is everything. 

### The Golden Rule of the Beach: Wait for the Sun to Dip

Do not go to the beach at 2:00 PM. The sand will be hot enough to burn your feet, and the UV index will be at its peak. 
*   **The Ideal Time:** Head to the beach around **4:30 PM to 5:00 PM**. 
*   **Where to Go:** The main *Swargadwar* beach area is iconic but very crowded. If your mother prefers a quieter environment, ask an E-rickshaw to drop you slightly further down the Marine Drive road (towards the Lighthouse area). It is cleaner and less chaotic.
*   **What to Do:** Rent a plastic chair (usually ₹20 - ₹50 per hour) from the vendors on the beach. Let your mother sit comfortably, feel the sea breeze, and watch the waves. 

### What to Eat at the Beach
As the evening sets in, the beach turns into a culinary hub.
*   **Jhal Muri & Chaat:** The local *Jhal Muri* (spicy puffed rice) is a must-try.
*   **Tea:** A hot cup of tea served in earthen pots (*kullhad*) by the sea is incredibly refreshing.
*   **A Word of Caution:** Avoid the raw/fried seafood stalls directly on the sand if you have a sensitive stomach, as hygiene standards vary wildly. Stick to hot, freshly made vegetarian snacks or packaged items.

## The Return Journey to Bhubaneswar

By 6:30 PM or 7:00 PM, exhaustion will likely set in, and it will be time to head back. 

### Why the Train Wins Again

Just as the train was the best option in the morning, it is the safest and most comfortable option for the night return. 
*   **Buses at Night:** While Mo Buses and private buses run late into the evening from the Puri Bus Stand, the highway traffic at night, combined with the glare of oncoming headlights and the jerky ride, is a terrible way to end a tiring day, especially for your mother who is prone to motion sickness.
*   **Evening Trains:** Head to the Puri Railway Station via an auto (₹50-₹80 from the beach). There are several convenient passenger and express trains departing for Bhubaneswar between 7:00 PM and 9:00 PM. 
    *   Example: The **Puri-Durg Express** or various passenger locals. Check the UTS app or IRCTC for the exact schedule on your phone while sitting at the beach.

### Arriving Back in Bhubaneswar

The train will drop you at the Bhubaneswar Railway Station in roughly 1.5 hours. 
*   From Master Canteen (the station square), you can easily book an Ola/Uber auto or hail a local auto-rickshaw back to **Sector 5, Lane 5, Niladri Vihar**.
*   Because it will be around 9:00 PM - 10:00 PM, the traffic in Bhubaneswar will be relatively light, ensuring a smooth final leg of your journey.

## Final Summary for the Perfect Day Trip

1.  **5:00 AM:** Auto from Niladri Vihar to Bhubaneswar Station.
2.  **6:45 AM:** Train (e.g., Durg Puri Exp) to Puri. (No motion sickness).
3.  **9:30 AM:** E-Rickshaw to the Bada Danda drop-off point.
4.  **10:00 AM:** Deposit phones/shoes at official lockers. Enter Temple. (Wear Cotton, carry Aadhaar).
5.  **10:30 AM - 12:30 PM:** Darshan (Use paid queue if available, politely refuse greedy pandas).
6.  **1:30 PM:** Buy and eat Mahaprasad at Ananda Bazar.
7.  **3:00 PM:** Visit Sudarshan Craft Museum for a quiet, shaded cultural break.
8.  **5:00 PM:** Relax on Puri Beach with tea.
9.  **7:30 PM:** Train back to Bhubaneswar.
10. **9:30 PM:** Auto back home to Niladri Vihar.

By following this guide, you bypass the worst of the tourist traps, protect your mother from the harsh weather and motion sickness, and ensure a deeply fulfilling, budget-friendly spiritual journey. 

*Jai Jagannath!*
